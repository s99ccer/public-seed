## Theoretical Mechanisms

### Model A: Regional Amplifiers
The 3 stations do not create the grid — they **amplify** the moon's trapping frequency regionally. Each station covers its continental zone:
- Rwenzori → Africa + Southern Europe
- Pyrenees → Western Europe + North Africa
- Mongolia → Asia + Eastern Europe

**Null zone**: Where no amplifier has coverage. The South Pacific (maximin point) is the largest such zone.

### Model B: Scalar Interference
The 3 stations emit phase-locked scalar waves. Scalar waves create standing wave interference patterns with nodes (null) and antinodes (peak). The 3 stations form a triangle that projects interference lobes across the planet.

The crude calculation (maximin point) identifies the primary antinode of destructive interference. However, subtler null zones may exist along great circle arcs connecting the stations.

### Model C: Topographic Shielding
Rather than planetary interference, the stations may have limited range (~5,000–8,000 km). Beyond this range, their effect attenuates. The South Pacific is beyond the range of all 3.

## Easter Island Connection
